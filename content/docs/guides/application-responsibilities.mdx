---
title: Application responsibilities
description: Understand the product, security, execution, and operational concerns outside Mosaic and Relay.
---

Mosaic and Relay intentionally stop short of being a complete trading platform.
The omitted responsibilities are not secondary; they are the areas most affected
by product design, providers, jurisdiction, and operating model.

## Responsibility map

| Layer | Primary responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| Relay | Normalize, cache, publish, hydrate, and transport market data |
| Mosaic | Form order intent and present accessible trading UI state |
| Host application | Identity, authorization, product policy, state orchestration, and execution |
| Provider adapters | Translate external protocols and payloads |
| Trading backend | Authoritative validation, preview, risk, routing, and order lifecycle |

## Authentication and authorization

Authenticate clients before attaching Relay sessions or accepting trading API
requests. Authorize every requested account, symbol, venue, and market-data
channel on the server.

Client-side visibility and disabled controls are not authorization mechanisms.

## Market-data entitlements

The application must enforce provider licensing and user entitlements. It also
owns public labelling for realtime, delayed, indicative, consolidated, and
synthetic data.

Do not forward provider credentials, private endpoints, internal service names,
or restricted source metadata through Relay messages or Mosaic display objects.

## Accounts and balances

Mosaic receives displayed cash and asset availability to improve order-entry
feedback. The backend remains authoritative for:

- account ownership and status
- settled and unsettled funds
- buying power and margin
- reservations from open orders
- position availability
- currency conversion
- custody and settlement constraints

Recalculate relevant state when previewing and confirming an order.

## Order preview and execution

Relay is not an order transport. Send Mosaic `OrderIntent` values to an
authenticated trading API.

The backend should:

1. parse and validate the submitted intent
2. load current account and product rules
3. apply permissions, risk, and compliance checks
4. obtain authoritative pricing and fees
5. bind any preview to the exact intent and user context
6. verify preview expiry at confirmation
7. route through a broker or venue adapter
8. persist and expose order lifecycle state

Never treat frontend validation or an estimated market reference as execution
authority.

## Compliance and policy

Depending on the product, the host may need suitability, appropriateness,
disclosures, jurisdiction restrictions, best-execution policy, surveillance,
record keeping, tax handling, and market-data agreements.

These requirements cannot be implemented safely as generic defaults in a UI or
market-data library.

## Precision and accounting

Mosaic and Relay currently use JavaScript numbers in display-oriented market
contracts. Execution, balances, ledger entries, fees, and accounting should use
the backend's decimal-safe representation and explicit currency precision.

Convert to display numbers only at a controlled boundary.

## Reliability and observability

The application owns:

- provider reconnect and failover
- cache and pub/sub availability
- hydration and subscription metrics
- stale-data detection
- order-book resynchronization
- structured logs and tracing
- rate limits and abuse controls
- graceful shutdown
- schema and protocol versioning

Test these paths with deterministic fake providers and sockets before relying on
credentialed smoke tests.

## Product presentation

Mosaic provides accessible behavior and styling slots, but the host controls
brand, responsive layout, disclosure placement, error tone, terminology, and
support paths.

The final application should make market-data state and execution uncertainty
understandable rather than merely technically correct.
